New York City consists of five boroughs—Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx, and Staten Island.
The Statue of Liberty was a gift from France in 1886 and stands on Liberty Island in New York Harbor.
New York City is the most populous city in the United States with over 8 million residents.
Over 800 languages are spoken in New York City, making it one of the most linguistically diverse cities in the world.
The city is famous for its skyline, including the Empire State Building, One World Trade Center, and the Chrysler Building.
Central Park spans 843 acres and is one of the most visited urban parks in the U.S.
New York City has the largest subway system in the world by number of stations, with 472 stations.
The Broadway Theater District in Manhattan is the heart of American theater, featuring many famous musicals.
The Metropolitan Museum of Art is one of the largest and most prestigious art museums in the world.
Wall Street is the global center of finance, housing the New York Stock Exchange.
NYC is often referred to as 'The Big Apple' and 'The City That Never Sleeps'.
Ellis Island was the gateway for over 12 million immigrants entering the U.S. between 1892 and 1954.
New York City is known for its diverse and iconic food offerings, from pizza and bagels to fine dining.
The Brooklyn Bridge, completed in 1883, connects Manhattan and Brooklyn and was the first steel-wire suspension bridge.
Times Square is famous for its bright lights, billboards, and the annual New Year's Eve ball drop.
NYC was originally called New Amsterdam when settled by the Dutch in 1624.
Over 100 million Americans can trace at least one ancestor to Ellis Island.
The New York Times, one of the world's most influential newspapers, was founded in 1851.
The National September 11 Memorial and Museum commemorates the victims of the 9/11 terrorist attacks.
The Metropolitan Museum of Art is the largest art museum in the U.S. with over 2 million works of art.
Lincoln Center is home to 11 performing arts organizations, including the New York Philharmonic and the Metropolitan Opera.
The United Nations headquarters is located in New York City, a hub for international diplomacy.
Grand Central Terminal, built in 1913, is one of the most famous train stations in the world.
Radio City Music Hall, opened in 1932, is known for its Rockettes and Christmas Spectacular show.
The Brooklyn Museum is one of the largest art museums in the U.S., housing extensive collections.
One World Trade Center, at 1,776 feet, is the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere.
The Empire State Building was the tallest building in the world until 1970.
The Chrysler Building is an Art Deco masterpiece and was briefly the tallest building in the world.
The Flatiron Building, completed in 1902, was one of NYC’s first skyscrapers.
The Woolworth Building was the tallest building in the world for 17 years after it was completed in 1913.
The Oculus, a modern transportation hub at the World Trade Center, was designed by Santiago Calatrava.
The High Line is an elevated park built on a former freight rail line on Manhattan’s West Side.
St. Patrick’s Cathedral, completed in 1878, is the largest Gothic-style Catholic cathedral in the U.S.
The Dakota Building, near Central Park, is famous as the home of John Lennon.
The New Year's Eve ball drop in Times Square started in 1907.
New York City has over 1,700 parks, playgrounds, and recreation facilities.
Central Park contains lakes, woodlands, a zoo, and a castle within its 843 acres.
The Bronx Zoo is the largest metropolitan zoo in the U.S., housing more than 6,000 animals.
Prospect Park in Brooklyn was designed by the same architects as Central Park and includes a zoo.
Battery Park, at the southern tip of Manhattan, offers views of the Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island.
Rockaway Beach in Queens is NYC’s only legal surfing beach.
The East River is a tidal estuary that separates Manhattan from Brooklyn and Queens.
The Hudson River runs along the west side of Manhattan and was historically a major route for trade.
Roosevelt Island, located between Manhattan and Queens, is accessible by tram.
Governors Island, off the southern tip of Manhattan, is now a public park and cultural space.
The New York Stock Exchange is the world’s largest stock exchange by market capitalization.
The Federal Reserve Bank of New York holds the largest gold reserves in the world.
New York City is governed by a mayor-council system with a strong mayor.
Tammany Hall was a political organization that dominated NYC politics in the 19th and early 20th centuries.
The Financial District, known as FiDi, is home to some of the world’s most powerful financial institutions.
Manhattan real estate is among the most expensive in the world.
The NYC Department of Education is the largest school district in the U.S., with over 1 million students.
NYC yellow taxis operate under a medallion system, which once saw medallions selling for over $1 million each.
Before the pandemic, NYC attracted over 65 million tourists annually.
As an Alpha++ city, NYC is a global center for finance, media, art, and culture.
The Statue of Liberty was declared a national monument in 1924.
Times Square was originally called Longacre Square before it was renamed in 1904.
Fifth Avenue is known for luxury shopping and landmarks like St. Patrick’s Cathedral.
Broadway is the center of American theater, home to numerous famous theaters and shows.
Rockefeller Center is famous for its ice skating rink, Christmas tree, and NBC headquarters.
Madison Square Garden is a world-famous venue for sports and concerts.
Carnegie Hall, opened in 1891, is one of the most prestigious venues for classical music.
The New York Public Library on Fifth Avenue is one of the largest public libraries in the world.
Union Square is a historic public square and a gathering spot for protests and markets.
Washington Square Park in Greenwich Village is famous for its arch and cultural scene.
New York-style pizza is famous for its thin crust.
New York bagels are considered some of the best due to the city's water quality.
Katz’s Delicatessen is famous for its pastrami on rye sandwiches.
NYC hot dogs are iconic, especially from street vendors and Nathan's Famous on Coney Island.
Manhattan’s Chinatown is the largest in the Western Hemisphere.

NYC’s halal food carts are famous for quick, tasty meals like gyros and falafel.
The Black and White cookie is a classic NYC dessert.

Classic cocktails like the Manhattan and Martini were invented in NYC.
NYC is one of the most filmed cities in the world.
Iconic TV shows like Friends, Seinfeld, and Sex and the City were set in NYC.
Manhattan is the most densely populated borough, with over 70,000 people per square mile.
Over 5 million people commute into NYC daily.
There are over 13,000 yellow taxis in NYC.

Nearly 40% of New Yorkers were born outside the U.S., contributing to its diversity.
